### Qiandong Miao written with Latin in 1957 orthography

![hms-Latn-1956](https://img.shields.io/badge/Writing_System-hms--Latn--1957-blue.svg)

Note that, in November 1950, five Hmong youths, Wu Diping (吴涤平), Pan Guangcheng (潘光成), Long Hede (隆和德), Zhang Shuchang (张树昌) and Tai Changhou (邰昌厚), who had been studying in Beijing, drew up an orthography for the Miao language Qiandong dialect written with Latin script after four months of study under the care and guidance of Wu Yuzhang (吴玉章) and Liang Juwu (梁聚伍). The writing system records the Qiandong Miao phonology based on the Northern subdialect (北部土语) in Taijiang county (台江县). The writing system is described in Zhou (2000) [^2], which suggests that this writing system uses all 24 Latin letters except ⟨<span class="latn">w</span>⟩ and ⟨<span class="latn">y</span>⟩, but the alphabet it provides does not correspond to the phonemes, and its correctness is questionable.

Note that, in 1952, Tai Changhou (邰昌厚) and Zhang Yongxiang (张永祥) produced Miao language textbooks in which an alphabet based written with Latin script was devised. The writing system records the Qiandong Miao phonology based on the Northern subdialect (北部土语) in Kaitang town (凯棠镇), Kaili county (凯里市). The writing system is described in Shi _et al._ (2021) [^3].

Note that, in 1956, the Scientific Symposium on Miao Language and Script Issues was held in Guiyang, Guizhou, and an orthography was formed by the Second Working Team of the Chinese Academy of Sciences. The writing system records the Qiandong Miao phonology based on the Northern subdialect (北部土语) in Yanghao village (养蒿村), Sankeshu town (三棵树镇), Kaili county (凯里市). No literature provides a complete alphabet, but incomplete inferences can be made from its relationship to 1957 orthography: (1) use ⟨<span class="latn">ə</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">eu</span>⟩ for _e_, use ⟨<span class="latn">æ</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">ae</span>⟩ for _ê_, use ⟨<span class="latn">ɂ</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">x</span>⟩ for _q̇_, use ⟨<span class="latn">x</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">hi</span>⟩ for _x_, use ⟨<span class="latn">ɣ</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">r</span>⟩ for _x́_, use ⟨<span class="latn">ʑ</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">n</span>⟩ for _ẓ_; (2) use ⟨<span class="latn">fh</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">fx</span>⟩ for _f̣_, use ⟨<span class="latn">sh</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">sx</span>⟩ for _ṣ_, use ⟨<span class="latn">lh</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">lx</span>⟩ for _ł̣_, use ⟨<span class="latn">shi</span>⟩ instead of ⟨<span class="latn">sxi</span>⟩ for _ṣ́_; (2) do not use ⟨<span class="latn">rh</span>⟩ for _r_; (3) use ⟨<span class="latn">c</span>⟩, ⟨<span class="latn">h</span>⟩, ⟨<span class="latn">k</span>⟩ and ⟨<span class="latn">s</span>⟩ for tones, but don’t know which tone that each letter corresponds to.

After the meeting, the Second Working Team discussed and amended the original scheme in the light of the delegates’ comments, and drafted an amended scheme. The writing system records the Qiandong Miao phonology based on the Northern subdialect (北部土语) in Yanghao village (养蒿村), Sankeshu town (三棵树镇), Kaili county (凯里市).

### Qiandong Miao written with Latin in 1958 orthography

![hms-Latn-1959](https://img.shields.io/badge/Writing_System-hms--Latn--1958-blue.svg)

The writing system uses the Latin script to record the Miao language Qiandong dialect. In late January 1958, the Guizhou Provincial Ethnic Languages Steering Committee and the Second Working Team convened the Miao language community of Guizhou Province to discuss modifications to the writing system. After the meeting, the 1957 orthography was revised. The writing system records the Qiandong Miao phonology based on the Northern subdialect (北部土语) in Yanghao village (养蒿村), Sankeshu town (三棵树镇), Kaili county (凯里市).
